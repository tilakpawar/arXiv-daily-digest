Propagation of waves in weakly ionized two-fluid plasmas. II. Nonlinear Alfvénic waves

David Martínez-Gómez

Eclipsing Binaries · Heartbeat & tidally distorted EBs Mass transfer & evolution in EBs · 2026-05-28

This study explores the propagation of nonlinear Alfvénic waves in weakly ionized plasmas, focusing on the influence of Hall's current and elastic collisions between ions and neutrals. The researchers derived analytical expressions to understand wave damping rates and heating processes, revealing significant polarization state dependencies. Numerical simulations further elucidated the generation of density perturbations and bulk flows.

Methods. The research utilized a two-fluid model to analyze both linearly and circularly polarized transverse waves in weakly ionized plasmas. Analytical expressions were derived for wave damping rates, heating processes, and nonlinear effects on plasma dynamics. Numerical simulations complemented the theoretical analysis by investigating specific scenarios of strong and weak collisional coupling.

  • Circularly polarized eigenmodes do not generate oscillatory second-order perturbations but still produce bulk flows.
  • The amplitude of longitudinal flow depends inversely on fluid density, providing insights into the first ionization potential (FIP) effect.
  • Wave energy dissipation and ponderomotive force have distinct impacts on charged versus neutral fluids in weak coupling conditions.

Conclusions. Understanding wave propagation dynamics in partially ionized plasmas enhances our comprehension of solar and stellar atmospheric processes, including heating mechanisms and the FIP effect. The findings highlight the importance of considering both collisional coupling strength and wave polarization state.

Why it matters. This research is crucial for EB/asteroseismology as it provides insights into plasma dynamics that can influence asteroseismic signals and the evolution of eclipsing binary systems, particularly in their lower atmospheric layers.
