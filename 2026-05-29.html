Tidally Excited Oscillations in the Heartbeat Binary KIC 1234567

A. Smith, B. Jones, C. Lee, D. Patel, E. Gomez, F. Ng et al.

Eclipsing binaries · text: arxiv-html · 2026-05-29

The authors analyse 4 years of Kepler photometry of an eccentric heartbeat binary, modelling the light curve with PHOEBE and extracting pulsation frequencies via iterative prewhitening.

  • Detect 12 tidally excited oscillation modes at exact harmonics of the orbital frequency
  • Derive component masses to 1.5% and radii to 2%
  • Find the dominant mode amplitudes vary on a ~200 day timescale

Conclusions. The system is a benchmark for tidal asteroseismology; mode amplitudes are consistent with resonance locking during orbital evolution.

Methods/data. Kepler long-cadence photometry; PHOEBE; iterative prewhitening

Why it matters. Links eclipsing-binary dynamical masses with asteroseismic mode identification in one object.
